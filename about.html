FROM SPACE
TO SCREEN

I trained as an architect but fell in love with how people navigate experiences — not just buildings. That pivot brought me to product design, where I apply spatial thinking, circulation logic, and narrative structure to every screen I touch. I've shipped work across Web3, SaaS, B2C, and AI — always asking the same question: does this feel right for the person using it?

01

Context before canvas

I research the problem space before opening Figma. Understanding who I'm designing for — and why — shapes every decision downstream.

02

Systems thinking at every scale

Whether it's a design token or an entire onboarding flow, I think in systems: what breaks, what scales, what holds meaning over time.

03

Emotion is the final metric

Usability is the baseline. The real goal is an experience that feels right — one that earns trust, sparks delight, or quietly disappears into flow.

MY JOURNEY

PILOT PHASE

I started with architecture, but what stayed with me wasn't buildings.
It was learning to ask why & understand users before designing.

REDEFINING SUSTAINABILITY

One project changed how I think about design.
The brief was a sustainable school.
But the real problem wasn't materials, it was access.
So we designed a mobile classroom and an app that helped teachers
reach students who couldn't commute. That project received a special
mention, but more importantly, it changed how I define impact.

DESIGNING FOR EXPERIENCE

For my thesis, I designed a Toy Museum.
Instead of a static space, I focused on circulation, how people move &
interact. The path itself became playful.
The users weren't just children. Adults mattered too.
I wanted them to feel nostalgic, not just informed.

SHIFTING SCALE

After working in architecture firms, I realised the building often felt like a
boundary.
What excited me more was designing systems and flows, not just physical
outcomes.

ENTERING PRODUCT DESIGN

That shift led to BanjarO., a gamified travel app.
I wanted travel to feel playful, not transactional.
Designing it made one thing clear: I wanted to move fully into product
design.

LEARNING TO SHIP AT SCALE

I joined Intract as a product design intern.
I worked on design systems, onboarding flows, Web3 products, AI tools,
and interactive landing pages, learning how real teams build, scale, and
ship.
It was intense, practical, and genuinely fun.

CONTINUING TO SHARPEN

Alongside this, I joined 10k Designers, worked on an AI movie app, & freelanced with Z42 Labs, building their website and learning Framer hands-on.
